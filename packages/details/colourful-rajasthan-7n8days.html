  • 2

    Day

    Udaipur
    Meal Plan: Breakfast-Lunch-Dinner

    Morning after breakfast proceed for sightseeing. The city of Udaipur is a lovely land. Pichola Lake derives its name from Pichola Village.This picturesque lake encloses the Jag Niwas Island and the Jag Mandir. Sajjan Niwas Garden is the largest garden of Rajasthan, sprawled over 100 acres of land. During 1850's, Maharana Sajjan Singh took the initiative to built this beautiful garden. Sajjan Niwas Bagh is celebrated for its numerous varieties of roses. Due to abundance of rose flowers, this garden is also known as Gulab Bagh or Rose Garden.. Fateh Sagar Lake, This delightful lake, bordered by hills and woodland was constructed by Maharana jai Singh to the north of Lake Pichola. It is an artificial lake dug up in 1678.The largest palace of the Udaipur is the City Palace , which was the winter palace of Maharana . The granite palace is a magical one. Stay overnight at the hotel in Udaipur.

  • 3

    Day

    Udaipur to Jodhpur (Approx 257 Km)
    Meal Plan: Breakfast-Lunch-Dinner

    After breakfast proceed for Jodhpur Sightseeing. Mehrangarh Fort, is one of the largest forts in forts. It is also the most magnificent fort in Jodhpur, infact, in the whole Rajasthan. The fort is amongst the popular tourist places in India. It is situated on a 150 m high hill. It was founded by Rao Jodha in 1459. The Mehrangarh Fort, with its beauty, is the living proof of the hard work and skill of the Jodhpuri sculptors. Seven gates have to be crossed to reach the fort.. Umaid Bhavan palace of Jodhpur, Rajasthan was built by Maharaja Umaid Singh. It is named after him only. It contains three hundred and forty seven rooms. Umaid Bhawan Palace was actually built for the welfare of the people. The purpose was to give employment to the people of Jodhpur during a long period of drought and famine. Stay overnight at the hotel in Jodhpur.

  • 4

    Day

    Jodhpur to Jaisalmer (Approx 278 Km)
    Meal Plan: Breakfast-Lunch-Dinner

    After Breakfast proceed to Jaisalmer and go for Desert Safari at SAM. Dinner at SAM desert Camp. Overnight stay in Hotel at Jaisalmer

  • 5

    Day

    Jaisalmer-Bikaner (Approx 331kms)
    Meal Plan: Breakfast-Lunch-Dinner

    After breakfast proceed for local sightseeing in Jaisalmer. Jaisalmer is known as The Golden city. The city of Jaisalmer has witnessed different forms of architecture in different eras. It is this era that saw the construction of most of the Havelis. Haveli is places, which served as the residence of Wazirs i.e. ministers and landlords. Usually they are very big and have lots of rooms and open space. The most famous among these is Patwon Ji ki Haveli. This Haveli was the first in Jaisalmer that is why it has greater significance. This Haveli became the pioneer of the constructions in Jaisalmer. Patwon Ji ki Haveli is actually the collection of 5 small Havelis.If you want to witness the continuity in design, you will have to visit Nathmal Ji ki Haveli..Last but not the least among major Havelis is Salim Ji ki haveli. Built in 1815, this Haveli is famous for its unique architecture based on the Peacock. Jaisalmer Fort is the most famous tourist attraction in Jaisalmer. It is a magnificent monument there. The Jaisalmer fort is the second oldest fort in the territories of Rajasthan. Jaisalmer fort is also famously or grandly called as the "Sonar Quila" or the Golden Fort. After sightseeing at Jaisalmer proceed to Bikaner. Overnight stay in Hotel at Bikaner.

  • 6

    Day

    Bikaner-Ajmer(Approx 270Kms/8 hours)
    Meal Plan: Breakfast-Lunch-Dinner

    After breakfast visit The Karni Mata Temple, the temple is dedicated to Karni Mata -an incarnation of Durga. The temple has fascinating huge silver gates and marble carvings that were donated by Maharaja Ganga Singhji. The most attractive feature of temple are the rodents who scamper freely within the premises and if one touches your feet its considered auspicious. They are regarded sacred and devotees buy prasad (feeding) to offer to them. Then visit Junagarh Fort. The wonderful architectural piece was built between 1588 -1593 AD by Raja Rai Singh, a General in army of Mughal emperor, Akbar. The imposing fort has 986 long wall with 37 bastions and two entrances. The Suraj Pole (Sun Gate) is main entrance to the fort. A measure feature of Junagarh fort is the superb quality of the stone carving-it rivals the best anywhere in the world. After visiting the places at Bikaner proceed to Jaipur. On the way visit Ajmer and Pushkar. Overnight stay in Hotel at Ajmer

  • 7

    Day

    Ajmer- Jaipur ( 130 kms/2 hours)
    Meal Plan: Breakfast-Lunch-Dinner

    Morning after breakfast proceed for sightseeing tour of Jaipur including, City Palace in the heart of Jaipur. Another monument near the City Palace, Jantar Mantar, is the largest of the five observatories built by Sawai Jai Singh. Visit the ancient capital of the Kachhawaha Rajputs, Amer fort. You can take the jeep ride upto the fort (on direct payment basis). Enroute enjoy a photo-stop at Hawa Mahal - the Palace of Winds and Jal Mahal. The rest of the day is free to explore this vibrant and colourful city and its local markets. Shopping in Jaipur is a colourful and dazzling experience with the wide range of handicrafts and jewellery to choose from. Overnight stay in Hotel at Jaipur.
