    Day

    NEW YORK CITY TOUR

    Cruise Tour to Liberty Island to visit Statue of Liberty

    Today begin your memorable journey through the United States of America. Start your day with a buffet breakfast at the hotel and soak in the sights of this magnificent New York City on a guided city tour. The sightseeing will start from the USA’s famous landmark “Statue of Liberty”. We will take a ferry ride from New Jersey to Liberty Island via Ellis Island to view the iconic and prestigious Statue of Liberty and a spectacular view of the New York City skyline. The copper statue of Liberty, designed by Frédéric Auguste Bartholdi, a French sculptor, was built by Gustave Eiffel and dedicated on October 28, 1886, as a gift to the United States from the people of France as a symbol of Freedom and Democracy. The statue was a welcoming sight to immigrants arriving from abroad with an overall height of 305 feet and 6 inches and the total weight of 225 tons. There are seven rays on her crown, one for each of the seven continents.

    New York City Tour

    Later we take a ferry from Liberty Island to Battery Park in New York. We explore the beautiful city of New York, see Downtown Wall Street, Central Park 5th Avenue, Midtown Manhattan, Rockefeller Centre, the headquarters of the United Nations, Brooklyn Bridge, Trump Hotel and lower Manhattan. Later we will spend some time at “Ground Zero” or 9/11 Memorial Plaza. The 9/11 Memorial Plaza is an 8-acre Park, where the World Trade Center Twin Towers once stood. The foundations of the Twin Towers have been converted into reflecting pools and the surrounding area is now a grove of trees. Here we visit the one World Trade Centre and Oculus, also called the World Trade Center Transportation Hub, the brilliantly white, space-aged looking structure.

    Unforgettable Moments in One World Observatory.

    After lunch, we visit the One World Trade Center is the main building of the rebuilt World Trade Center complex in Lower Manhattan, New York City. One World Trade Center is the tallest building in the United States, the tallest building in the Western Hemisphere, and the seventh-tallest in the world. One World Observatory is at the top of the One WTC. The Observatory is located on the 100th, 101st, and 102nd floors and it offers views of New York City's iconic sights, surrounding waters and skyline. Later you will have a buffet local dinner at the restaurant.

    Visit Times Square and be amazed by the life and light of the New York City.

    New York City that never sleeps. After dinner, you visit Times Square and be amazed by the life and light of the New York City at night. This is one of the most famous intersections in the world. During the 364 days of the year, Times Square is almost as crowded, as street performers put on shows and visitors from around the world gawk at the millions of lights that adorn the neon billboards. It offers non-stop events and activities that reflect the spirit of the Big Apple and you will never far from the festivals, parades, entertainment, major sporting events and celebrations of art and culture. Free Time at very live Time Square. Later you will back to the hotel. Overnight stay in Hotel Fairfield Marriott NJ or similar in New York.

    NEW YORK – WASHINGTON D.C.

    After an American breakfast at the hotel check out and drive to Washington D.C.Washington DC is the United States’ capital city on the Potomac River, bordering the states of Maryland and Virginia. On arrival you will meet the guide and visit the major sights of Washington D.C. like the White House, Capitol Hill, the home of the United States Congress, and the seat of Supreme Court, Lincoln Memorial, Washington Monument, Jefferson Memorial, WW2 Memorial, Korean War Memorial, Martin Luther Memorial, FBI building and Washington National Cathedral, Pentagon ………..etc.

    Visit Smithsonian National Air and Space Museums

    After lunch visit the Smithsonian Air and Space Museum. The National Air and Space Museum of the Smithsonian holds the largest collection of historic aircraft and spacecraft in the world. On July 20, 1969, astronauts Neil Armstrong and Buzz Aldrin landed in the Lunar Module Eagle and became the first humans to step foot on the lunar surface while astronaut Michael Collins orbited above inside the Command Module, Columbia. Here we can see the collection of objects from that historic mission. The Human Spaceflight collection from the first U.S. manned Mercury missions through Apollo, the Space Shuttle, and the International Space Station…etc. It was established in 1946 and the museum saw approximately 6.7 million visitors, making it the fifth most visited museum in the world. Tonight, enjoy an Italian/ Chinese buffet dinner and later transfer to the hotel. Overnight stay in Sheraton College Park or similar in Washington DC.

    WASHINGTON – NIAGARA

    Hershey’s Chocolate Factory visit

    After an American breakfast at the hotel, check out and drive to Harrisburg. On arrival, we visit Hershey’s Chocolate Park. Hershey’s is widely popular for Chocolate -Themed Park in United States. Hershey's chocolates were founded by candy magnate Milton S. Hershey. Ride a train that follows the journey of Chocolate Tour, an attraction that allows visitors to trace the production from cocoa bean to the final product. The carriage takes you through a replica factory and explains what goes into making chocolate so tasty. Enjoy a taste of the chocolate after the tour. This ride is an original attraction, showing a simulation of the chocolate-making process. Free time to buy Hershey’s Chocolate to your home. Then you will continue to drive through the lush green valley and enjoy some stunning scenery on the way as you arrive at Niagara Falls, the natural wonders of the world. On arrival, you will transfer to the hotel. Evening free time to view the Falls. Dinner and overnight stay in Wyndham Garden Niagara Fallsview or similar in Niagara.

    Day

    NIAGARA FALLS

    Maid of the Mist Boat Ride to Niagara Falls

    After the continental breakfast proceed to the natural wonders east of the Mississippi, the Niagara Falls, the greatest. The majesty, colors and roar of one of the Great Lakes dropping into the next are impossible to describe. See both the Horseshoe, American and Bridal Veil Falls this morning as you cruise the thrilling the Maid of the Mist boat ride for an unbelievable view of all the three Falls(seasonal). The boat starts at a calm part of the Niagara River, near the Rainbow Bridge, and takes you to the American and Bridal Veil Falls, then into the dense mist of spray inside the curve of the Horseshoe Falls. Feel the mist as this boat takes you to the base of the Horseshoe Falls. Rainbows are usually visible day and night. Freshen up after your damp ride.

    The Cave of the Winds

    After the lunch, the Cave of the Winds trip takes you closer to the waters of Niagara Falls than you thought possible. A journey that takes you deep below and behind the heart of the Niagara Falls. You will descend 175 feet (53 meters) deep by elevators through bedrock to tunnels that lead to viewing portals which are one-third of the way behind the massive Niagara Falls. Then, clad in a bright yellow poncho and wearing the special footwear provided, you follow a tour guide over a series of wooden walkways to the famous “Hurricane Deck”. The rushing waters loom above you, dousing you with a generous spray as you face the thundering Falls head-on. Enjoy your time in Niagara Falls with the breathtaking Falls, Hurricane Decks, Cave of the Winds and the Maid of the Mist and more, a Niagara Falls vacation is the ultimate. Later transfer to hotel and free time at the hotel.

    Niagara Falls in Lights

    Later after dinner you will get an opportunity to witness the Niagara Falls in Lights. Niagara Falls is transformed into incredible, multi-colored water and light masterpiece. Special lights are housed in the Illumination Tower with new LED lighting technology at the brink of the falls. The LED lighting provides programmable lighting and a wider spectrum of color. Together, these lights work to create a breathtaking view of the Niagara Falls. Later return to the hotel. Overnight stay in Wyndham Garden Niagara Falls view or similar in Niagara.

On arrival in Orlando airport, you will transfer to Kennedy Space Center (NASA) Kennedy Space Center (KSC) is named after President John F. Kennedy, who famously declared in 1961 that the United States would put an astronaut on the moon. You will transfer to an air-conditioned motor coach and go beyond the Kennedy Space Center Bus Tour with the Explore Tour, making several stops to capture photo opportunities and view icons of NASA spaceflight operations. Spend hours exploring the NASA world-class facility, launch pads and rocket exhibits like Saturn V. We can also visit the place where Apollo, Skylab and Space Shuttle programs were carried out from Kennedy Space Center and first moon mission launch pad and control stations. You will get a chance visit Moon Mission Zone and experience the historic moments in the Firing Room Theater and the Lunar Theater and the famous Space Shuttle Atlantis.     HOOVER DAM & GRAND CANYON

    After an American breakfast drive through the desert, passing through Joshua tree forests and the Mojave Desert, and reach the famous Hoover Dam, built 1933 in Colorado river. This impressive engineering marvel spans approximately 245 square miles of surface area and took 3.25 million yards of concrete to construct. The Hoover Dam continues to play an essential role for the surrounding economies and their inhabitants, providing water to 8,000,000 people in Arizona, California, and Nevada and especially to Las Vegas. During this tour of the Hoover Dam, enjoy the best views from both the Arizona and Nevada sides of the Colorado River. Pat Tillman Memorial Bridge also the major attraction, offers the highest accessible overlook of the Dam and Black Canyon.

    Visit Grand Canyon, the natural wonders of the world

    Later you will transfer to the Grand Canyon, another natural wonder of the world. The Grand Canyon is a steep-sided canyon carved by the Colorado River in the state of Arizona in the United States. Nearly two billion years of earth's geological history have been exposed as the Colorado River cut their channels through layer after layer of rock while the Colorado Plateau was uplifted. The Grand Canyon is 446 km long, up to 29 km wide and attains a depth of over a mile. Several recent studies support the hypothesis that the Colorado River established its course through the area about 5 to 6 million years ago. Since that time, the Colorado River has driven the down-cutting of the tributaries and retreat of the cliffs, simultaneously deepening and widening the canyon. For thousands of years, the area has been continuously inhabited by Native Americans, who built the settlements within the canyon and its many caves.

    You will get a chance to visit Grand Canyon West Rim with Skywalk. A breathtaking view of Grand Canyon from a man-made glass bridge will serve up quite a thrill as you take in the views of the Canyon. The Grand Canyon West Rim 3,500 plus feet high above the Colorado River by taking a stroll on the world-famous Skywalk.Later transfer to the hotel. Later proceed to the hotel. Dinner and overnight stay in Hotel Luxor or similar in Las Vegas.

    After continental breakfast check out hotel and proceed to San Francisco.San Francisco is the prettiest and most high-tech city in the world surrounded by the Pacific Ocean and San Francisco Bay. It’s the birthplace of the League of Nations and United Nations and known for iconic Golden Gate Bridge and colorful Victorian houses. On arrival we meet guide and visit Lombard Street, Pier 39, Nob Hill, Civic Centre, Union Square, Fisherman’s Wharf, League of Nations buildings and other highlights....etc.

    Explore the beauty of Golden Gate Bridge from Bay Cruise

    Later you will transfer to San Francisco Bay Cruise to enjoy the beauty and magic of the San Francisco Bay on an exhilarating one-hour Bay Cruise. The San Francisco Bay is a shallow water from California and passes through the bay to the Pacific Ocean. The waterway entrance to San Francisco Bay from the Pacific Ocean is called the Golden Gate. The Golden Gate Bridge is an iconic structure connecting the city of San Francisco to Marin County, California. It spans almost two miles across the Golden Gate, the narrow strait where San Francisco Bay opens to meet the Pacific Ocean. The dream of connecting San Francisco to its northern neighbors became a reality when construction commenced in 1933. The Golden Gate Bridge, opened to the public in 1937, has endured as a picture-perfect landmark and an engineering marvel.You get an opportunity to explore the beauty of the Golden Gate Bridge from bay Cruise.

    Explore the beauty of San Fransisco from Twin Peaks

    After the City Tour, we will proceed to the Twin Peaks. The Twin Peaks are two prominent hills with an elevation of about 925 feet located near the geographic center of San Francisco, California. We can enjoy the beauty of San Fransicso from Twin Peaks. A grassy 64-acre hilltop park is a popular attraction, with its hiking trails leading up to wind-swept peaks and 360-degree views of the Bay Area.Twin peaks named for a pair of 922-foot-high summits, is a remote residential neighborhood with modern homes densely packed on steep lots along winding streets.
